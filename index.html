Mary Anning (1799-1847)

"Over two hundred years ago, a young girl discovers a fossil, and begins a lifelong vocation that earns her a place in history."

Life history of Mary Anning:

  • 1799 - Mary Anning was born in Lyme Regis (Dorset), England as 5th child in a very poor carpenter family
  • November 1810 - Father died of tuberculosis. After their father's death Mary and her brother Joseph started to collect fossils, mainly from the Triassic and Jurasic Period, which they could sell to tourists to earn some extra money.
  • 1811 - The discovery of an almost complete skeleton of an Ichthyosaur, a large marine reptile, attracted the attention of the geological scientific community.
  • 1823 - Discovery of first complete skeleton of a Plesiosaurus
  • 1828 - First British finding of a pterosaur or "flying dragon".
  • 1829 - Discovery of a Squaloraja fish skeleton
  • 1839 - Mary published a short comment in the Journal of Natural History, vol. III.
  • 1835 - Awarded with an annuity by the British Association for the Advancement of Science and the British government, in return for her many contributions to the science of geology.
  • 9 March 1847 - Mary Anning died by the effects of breast cancer.
